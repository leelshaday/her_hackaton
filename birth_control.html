Birth Control

WHAT IS BIRTH CONTROL?

Birth control (also called contraception) is how you prevent pregnancy. There are many different types of birth control. Birth control only works when you use it correctly. Take the time to understand how each type of birth control works and how you use it. If you're sexually active and don't want a baby, use birth control. A pregnancy can happen any time you have unprotected sex.

Different approaches to birth control

A good way to narrow down your birth control options is to figure out which method is right for you. Generally speaking, there are three ways in which you can prevent an unplanned pregnancy:

  1. Barrier birth control: any method that prevents sperm from reaching, and fertilizing, an egg falls under barrier control. Included in this category are condoms, diaphragms, cervical caps, intrauterine devices (IUDs), tubal ligation, and sponges.
  2. Hormonal birth control: another approach for preventing pregnancy is to regulate your ovulation, which we can do with hormonal medications.
  3. Natural birth control: if you’d prefer a more natural approach to birth control, you can try abstinence, withdrawal, and fertility awareness.

Efficacy is important

If you want true protection, it helps to understand the efficacy rating of the birth control option you’re considering. To give you an idea, here’s a sample:

  • IUD — 99%
  • Implant — 99%
  • Injections — 96%
  • Condoms — 87%
  • Sponges — 78-86%
  • Withdrawal — 78%
  • Fertility awareness — 77-98%
